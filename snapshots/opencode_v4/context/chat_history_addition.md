# Additional Context Not Covered by My Current "About Me"

The existing document already covers my work environment, technical constraints, and immediate goals.

I'm looking for additional long-term context that helps an AI assistant better understand how I think and make decisions.

Potential missing areas include:

---

## Thinking Style

* I naturally think in terms of systems rather than isolated problems.
* I enjoy understanding how things work internally instead of treating them as black boxes.
* I learn primarily by building rather than reading.
* When I encounter friction, repetitive work, or unnecessary complexity, my instinct is to understand why it exists and whether it can be eliminated.

---

## Builder Mindset

* I enjoy creating tools almost as much as using them.
* If a tool doesn't exist, is too expensive, or doesn't fit my workflow, my instinct is often to build one.
* I have practical experience beyond software, including mechanical work, welding, machining, woodworking, electrical work, hydraulics, pneumatics, and general troubleshooting.
* I am comfortable combining software and hardware to solve problems.

---

## Personal Philosophy

Some recurring themes that seem to influence many of my decisions:

* I value long-term leverage over short-term convenience.
* I prefer understanding over blindly using a solution.
* I prefer owning my tools whenever practical (local-first, self-hosted, open source).
* I am willing to invest significant effort upfront if it compounds over years.
* I care about engineering elegance, but I recognize that perfectionism and over-engineering can become obstacles to actually finishing projects.

---

## Decision-Making Preferences

When several reasonable solutions exist, I tend to optimize for some combination of:

* long-term usefulness
* maintainability
* cost effectiveness
* automation potential
* reliability
* local-first operation
* simplicity

I generally prefer mature, well-supported technologies unless newer options provide a compelling advantage.

---

## Constraints Beyond Work

Time and money are my primary personal constraints.

Because of this:

* projects are frequently paused and resumed later
* requirements evolve while I continue thinking about the problem
* I sometimes redesign systems before Version 1 is complete

An AI assistant should help me distinguish between worthwhile architectural improvements and unnecessary redesign.

---

## Personal Technology Environment

Outside work I generally prefer:

* Linux-first workflows
* self-hosted services
* open-source software
* automation over manual processes
* owning my own infrastructure when practical

---

## Cost Philosophy

Cost effectiveness is extremely important.

I prefer tools that:

* are free or inexpensive
* pay for themselves
* provide exceptional long-term value

I strongly dislike recurring subscriptions unless they clearly justify their ongoing cost.

I have no problem paying a premium for high-quality tools that save meaningful time or significantly improve my capabilities.

---

## How AI Should Collaborate With Me

Rather than simply answering questions, an assistant should:

* challenge my assumptions when appropriate
* point out existing tools before I reinvent them
* explain important tradeoffs instead of giving a single recommendation
* favor practical implementation over abstract theory
* recognize when I may be optimizing prematurely
* help me identify when "good enough" is better than continuing to redesign

---

## Personal Values

Some values that seem to consistently guide my projects:

* Reduce friction.
* Build reusable systems.
* Prefer solutions that compound over time.
* Optimize for long-term ownership instead of short-term convenience.
* Keep complexity proportional to the problem.
* Automate repetitive work whenever the investment makes sense.
